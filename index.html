step 1

The series of tasks in this exercise will give a basic introduction into the functionality of the QGIS software and provide the practical and necessary skills in constructing land cover statistics for their country or area of interest. The exercises will use open source publicly available software, vector and raster datasets. For this exercise, the country of Fiji.will be used, participants can choose their country of choice if they wish, but should be aware that depending on the size of the country chosen processing time will also increase.The result will be individual land cover spatial datasets from 1995 to 2018 at 300 meters resalution.
The First Step of in this section shows how to downlode and install the QGIS Desktop linkby following the bellow video


step 2

Downloderaster and vector data for this project.Data for this lab will be both in raster (cell based) and vector format(polygon). The fiji administreative boundaries data sets is used as the example country for this exercise.uses global land cover raster datasets produced by the European Space Agency (ESA) as part of their Climate Change Initiative project and are the result of the compilation and reprocessing of 5 different satellite missions. Output is an annual time series of global land cover data at the 300-metre resolution from 1995 to 2015.

In this step we will open QGIS Desktop with GRASS and load the raster (ESA global land Cover) From 1995 to 2015 in to QGIS and vector data of FIJI adm0 in to QGIS. Clip the (ESA global land Cover) data sets to the Fijiadm0 shape File data sets.

The bellow Example Video shows how to Clip the FIJI administrative boundaries data for years 2005 and 2015. By using the below Video as a reference and Clip the Fiji (ESA global land Cover) from year 1995 to 2015.
